The vibrance and diversity of Johannesburg

Johannesburg, South Africa's largest city, was established in 1886 due to a gold rush. Its vibrant diversity reflects a mix of ethnic communities and cultures.

Historically significant in the anti-apartheid struggle, Johannesburg saw major events like the 1976 Soweto Uprising. In the post-apartheid era, the city has evolved into a major economic hub, known for its dynamic arts scene and cultural landmarks such as the Apartheid Museum.

Modern neighborhoods like Maboneng and Braamfontein highlight its innovative spirit, offering a rich blend of cuisines, entertainment, and cultural experiences.

Things to do in Jozi

Visit Nelson Mandela's home

Nelson Mandela's former home is a historic landmark and museum celebrating his legacy.

Mandela lived here with his family from 1946 to 1962, and the house now offers visitors a glimpse into his life and the broader anti-apartheid struggle.

Take a trip to the zoo

The Johannesburg Zoo, established in 1904, is a renowned attraction offering a delightful escape into nature.

With its lush landscapes and educational programs, the Jo'burg Zoo is a must-visit destination for animal lovers and families alike.

Bungee off the Soweto towers

The bungee jump off the Soweto Cooling Towers is an exhilarating adventure for thrill-seekers.

Located at the iconic towers, once used for cooling in Johannesburg's power plants, this bungee jump offers a unique view of the vibrant Soweto neighborhood.
