The vibrance and diversity of Johannesburg

Johannesburg, South Africa's largest city, was established in 1886 due to a gold rush. Its vibrant diversity reflects a mix of ethnic communities and cultures.

Historically significant in the anti-apartheid struggle, Johannesburg saw major events like the 1976 Soweto Uprising. In the post-apartheid era, the city has evolved into a major economic hub, known for its dynamic arts scene and cultural landmarks such as the Apartheid Museum.

Modern neighborhoods like Maboneng and Braamfontein highlight its innovative spirit, offering a rich blend of cuisines, entertainment, and cultural experiences.

Things to do in Jozi

Visit Nelson Mandela's home

Nelson Mandela's former home at 8115 Vilakazi Street in Soweto is a historic landmark and museum celebrating his legacy. Mandela lived here with his family from 1946 to 1962, and the house now offers visitors a glimpse into his life and the broader anti-apartheid struggle.

The museum features exhibits on Mandela's personal and political journey, highlighting his role in South Africa's fight for freedom. Located on Vilakazi Street, the only street in the world where two Nobel Peace Prize winners lived, Mandela's home is a UNESCO World Heritage site and a powerful symbol of hope and resilience.

Take a trip to the zoo

The Johannesburg Zoo, established in 1904, is a renowned attraction offering a delightful escape into nature.

Located in the heart of the city, it houses over 2,000 animals across diverse habitats. Visitors can explore exhibits featuring African wildlife, including lions, giraffes, and rhinos, as well as exotic species from around the world.

The zoo is committed to conservation and education, providing engaging experiences for all ages. With its lush landscapes and educational programs, the Johannesburg Zoo is a must-visit destination for animal lovers and families alike.

Bungee off the Soweto towers

The bungee jump off the Soweto Cooling Towers is an exhilarating adventure for thrill-seekers.

Located at the iconic towers, once used for cooling in Johannesburg's power plants, this bungee jump offers a unique view of the vibrant Soweto neighborhood.

Standing 100 meters high, the jump provides a breathtaking free-fall experience combined with panoramic city views. It's not just an adrenaline rush but also a chance to engage with Johannesburg's rich history and dynamic urban landscape.
